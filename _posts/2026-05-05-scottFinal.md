What if the most significant communications revolution in history wasn't the printing press, or the internet, or even writing itself? I'd argue it was something quieter: the invention of the codex, the bound book, and its adoption by the three great Abrahamic religions. This episode makes the case for why the codex changed not just how we stored information, but how entire civilizations organized truth, power, and belief.
