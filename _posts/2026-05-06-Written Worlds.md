## Podcast Introduction

In this episode, I argue that the invention of writing is the most significant communication revolution in human history. By comparing Mesopotamian cuneiform with early Mesoamerican writing systems like the Olmec and Maya, I explore how writing reshaped human thought, structured knowledge, and laid the foundation for modern communication—including today’s digital world.

## Early Writing Technology

![Early cuneiform tablet recording beer rations](https://smarthistory.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/121754001-1536x1251.jpg)

*Early writing tablet recording the allocation of beer, 3100–3000 B.C.E., Late Prehistoric period, clay, probably from southern Iraq. (© Trustees of the British Museum). This tablet shows early pictographs used to track goods like beer rations, along with numerical impressions. Over time, these symbols became more abstract and developed into cuneiform writing.*
